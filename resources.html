★ Seasonal Checklist

Storm Prep & Post-Storm Checklist

⚡ Before A Storm

  • Inspect gutters and downspouts — remove any debris
  • Trim tree branches overhanging the roof
  • Check attic for signs of previous leaks (water stains, mold)
  • Know your insurance policy — deductible amount and coverage type
  • Photograph your roof from the ground for insurance records
  • Have our number saved: (502) 919-0278

🌧️ After A Storm

  • Check for missing, lifted, or cracked shingles from the ground
  • Inspect gutters for granule buildup (sign of hail damage)
  • Check for dents on gutters, downspouts, AC unit, or vents
  • Look in the attic for new leaks or daylight
  • Call us for a FREE professional inspection before filing a claim
  • Document everything with photos before any cleanup

★ Know The Terms

Roofing Glossary

Decking
The structural wood base beneath your shingles — also called roof sheathing. Damage here requires immediate repair.
Flashing
Metal strips that seal joints around chimneys, vents, and valleys. Failed flashing is a top cause of leaks.
Ice & Water Shield
A self-adhesive waterproof membrane installed at eaves and valleys to prevent water intrusion during ice dams or driving rain.
Drip Edge
Metal trim installed at roof edges that directs water into gutters and away from the fascia.
Ridge Cap
Specially shaped shingles installed along the peak of the roof to protect and seal the ridge line.
Granules
The mineral coating on asphalt shingles that protects from UV and weather. Granule loss = roof aging.
Soffit & Fascia
The horizontal (soffit) and vertical (fascia) boards under the roof overhang. Often damaged in storms.
Replacement Cost Value
Insurance coverage that pays for a new roof at today's prices — vs. Actual Cash Value (ACV), which deducts depreciation.

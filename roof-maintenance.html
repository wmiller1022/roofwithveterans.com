★ The Reality

Why Roofs Need Maintenance

  • Debris buildup causes water to back up under shingles
  • Pipe boot flashings are known to fail before the life of the roof
  • Nail pops caught early are easy fixes — left alone, they cause pricey leaks
  • Loose shingles get blown off; hand-sealed shingles stay put
  • Exposed fasteners need re-caulking roughly every 5 years
  • Blown-out gutters need debris cleared so water can flow
  • Simple repairs of a few shingles, paired with maintenance, extend the roof's life

★ The Maintenance Schedule

What We Recommend

5

1st Maintenance — Year 5

Check boots, flashings, and shingle seal. Remove debris and re-caulk exposed fasteners.

10

2nd Maintenance — Year 10

If the first pass was done, repeat the same service to keep everything sealed and protected.

15

3rd Maintenance — Year 15

If the first two were done. After year 15, maintain every other year to maximize roof life. Expect yearly service after year 25 for asphalt shingles.

After year 15 we recommend every-other-year service unless we suggest otherwise — every year once you pass 25 years on asphalt shingles.
